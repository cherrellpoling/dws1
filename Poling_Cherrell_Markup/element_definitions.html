Element Definitions

<h1>
This is the largest font header, used for the main title.
<h2>
This is the 2nd largest subheading.
<h3>
This is the 3rd largest subheading.
<h4>
This is the 4th largest subheading.
<h5>
This is the 5th largest subheading.
<h6>
This is the 6th largest subheading.
<p>
Creates a new paragraph for text.
<b>
Makes characters appear bold.
<i>
Makes characters appear italic.
<sup>
Makes characters superscript or raised on the line.
<sub>
Makes characters subscript or below the line.
<br/>
Creates a break on a line, starts a new line of text.
<hr/>
Creates a horizontal line
<strong>
Used to indicate importance, or another way to bold.
<em>
Used for subtle emphasis that change sentence meaning, or another way to italicize.
<blockquote>
Centers quotes that take up an entire paragraph.
<q>
Used to quote a line of text within a sentence. Not used for IE.
<abbr>
Used to create abbreviations and acronyms that can be used and referred back to.
<cite>
Used when making a reference. Can be used for research papers.
<dfn>
Used when defining a new term.
<address>
Used to contact contaction information of the author.
<ins>
Indicates an insertion of text with an underline.
<del>
Indicates a deletion of text with a cross through.
<s>
Indicates wrong information that cannot be deleted with a cross through.
<ol>
Ordered List. Items on this list are numbered.
<li>
List Item. This is the actual item on the list.
<ul>
Unordered List. Listed with bullets.
<dl>
Used to indicate a Definition List.
<dt>
Defined Term. Indicates the term to be defined.
<dd>
Defined Definition. The definition of the Defined Term.
